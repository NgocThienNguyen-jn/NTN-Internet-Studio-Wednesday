Ambrosial Feast of the Hero

A Divine Feast with the Legendary Champions of Olympus

Zeus

Zeus is the king of the gods, ruling over Olympus with thunderbolts in hand. His presence at the banquet represents power, justice, and the authority of the heavens. I invited him because beyond the fearsome storms, I believe he carries wisdom about leadership and balance. Sharing a meal with Zeus would be a chance to see the god not only as a ruler, but as a storyteller of the skies.

Poseidon

Poseidon, god of the seas, commands the waves and stirs the storms. His spirit is unpredictable, shifting between calm waters and violent tides. I invited him because the oceans cover most of our world, and no one knows their secrets better than him. At the banquet, his tales of sea creatures, sunken cities, and the endless horizon would make the night both exciting and full of mystery.

Hades

Hades, lord of the underworld, is often misunderstood as grim or distant, but he rules his realm with fairness and dignity. Unlike his brothers, he governs the unseen, where every soul eventually journeys. I invited him because his perspective on life and death would add depth to the gathering. His wisdom about fate, legacy, and the meaning of mortality would give the banquet a profound and thoughtful atmosphere.
