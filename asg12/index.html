My Favorite Movies

Movies have always been my favorite way to escape, learn, and just feel something real for a couple of hours. This little gallery is basically a collection of films that stuck with me for different reasons. Some made me cry, some made me think, and some just reminded me how cool storytelling can be. They come from different countries and styles, but each one has something special that made me love it. So if you’re looking for a new favorite or just want to peek into the stuff I enjoy, welcome to my movie corner.

Bo Gia

This movie follows Ba Sang, a father trying his best to hold his family together in a crowded alley in Saigon. The story shows how misunderstandings and money stress can pull people apart, but also how love always sits quietly in the background. It mixes humor, sadness, and a lot of relatable family moments. You should watch it if you want something heartfelt that feels close to real life.

Toi Thay Hoa Vang Tren Co Xanh

Set in a peaceful countryside village in the 80s, the movie follows two brothers as they grow up, deal with jealousy, experience first love, and face the ups and downs of childhood. The whole film feels like a memory you want to keep forever. The visuals are beautiful and the nostalgic feeling is strong, so it is definitely worth watching if you love stories that remind you of simpler days.

Mat Biec

This film tells the story of Ngạn and Hà Lan, childhood friends who drift into a complicated love as they grow older. It is a story filled with longing, heartbreak, and choices that change everything. The soft colors and dreamy visuals make every scene feel emotional. Watch it if you enjoy love stories that are gentle, beautiful, and a little painful in a good way.

Hai Phuong

The movie follows a mother whose daughter is kidnapped, and she goes on an intense chase through the criminal world to get her back. The action scenes are fast and raw, and you can feel every punch because the stakes are so personal. It stands out as one of the strongest Vietnamese action films. You should watch it if you want something exciting that still has a powerful emotional core.

Spirited Away

The story follows Chihiro, a young girl who gets trapped in a spirit world after her parents are turned into pigs. She has to work at a strange bathhouse while trying to find a way to save her family. The world building is full of imagination and beautiful details that make everything feel alive. You should watch it because it captures the feeling of wonder and bravery in a way few films can.

A Silent Voice

A Silent Voice follows a boy who used to bully a deaf girl when they were kids. Years later, he wants to make things right and understand the pain he caused. The story deals with guilt, forgiveness, and learning how to accept yourself. It is emotional and very human. You should watch it if you appreciate films that feel honest and deeply moving.

Your Name

Your Name is about a boy in Tokyo and a girl in a quiet rural town who mysteriously start switching bodies. They try to understand what is happening, and along the way they form a connection that goes far deeper than they expect. It is visually stunning and has a soundtrack that brings out all the emotion. You should watch it if you want something romantic, magical, and unforgettable.

Weathering With You

This movie is about a runaway boy in Tokyo who meets a girl with the power to change the weather. As they grow closer, they discover that her ability comes with a cost. The film blends romance and fantasy with gorgeous scenery and music. You should watch it if you like stories that mix youthful love with big emotional moments.

Parasite

Parasite is about a poor family that slowly gets hired by a wealthy family, pretending not to know each other. Things start out funny and clever, but everything becomes darker once their hidden secrets begin to unravel. It is a smart look at class, wealth, and how far people will go to survive. You should watch it because it is full of surprises and every scene has something to think about.

Train to Busan

The story takes place on a train during a sudden zombie outbreak. A father and his young daughter are stuck on board with strangers as the world falls apart outside. The tension is constant, but the emotional moments hit just as hard as the action. You should watch it if you want a movie that is intense, scary, and unexpectedly touching at the same time.

Decision to Leave

This movie follows a detective who becomes drawn to a mysterious woman connected to a murder case. As he gets more involved, the line between love and suspicion gets blurry. The film is slow, thoughtful, and full of emotion that is not always spoken out loud. Watch it if you enjoy mysteries that focus more on feeling and atmosphere than big twists.

The Handmaiden

The Handmaiden takes place in the 1930s and follows a rich heiress who forms a complicated connection with the woman hired to deceive her. The story is full of secrets, shifting perspectives, and moments that make you rethink everything you just watched. It is stylish, beautiful, and incredibly engaging. You should watch it if you like films that play with your expectations in clever ways.
