Ancient House Hanoi

Hanoi

Dine in pure elegance at Ancient House Hanoi, housed within a meticulously preserved 1920s French colonial merchant house in the heart of the Old Quarter. This exceptional restaurant transports guests back to the romantic era of French Indochina while celebrating the sophisticated cuisines of Northern Vietnam. The intimate setting, adorned with period antiques and flickering candlelight, creates an unforgettable ambiance for culinary discovery.

The menu showcases Northern Vietnamese regional specialties rarely found outside of family homes. Their famous egg coffee, a Hanoi institution, combines rich espresso with whipped egg yolk and condensed milk‚Äîa taste of Vietnam's unique coffee culture. Main courses feature Hanoi's most celebrated dishes: soft crab in tamarind sauce, grilled fish wrapped in clay, and the legendary Hanoi ph·ªü with beef tendon. Each dish represents recipes passed down through generations of Hanoian families.

The restaurant's wine selection pairs beautifully with the refined Vietnamese cuisine, and the attentive staff moves gracefully through candlelit rooms, ensuring every need is anticipated. From the moment you enter the heavy wooden doors of this colonial mansion to the moment you leave, you're transported to Vietnam's golden age of elegance and refinement. Riverside Elegance - Mekong Delights

Can Tho, Mekong Delta

Experience the magic of the Mekong Delta from a unique floating restaurant, Mekong Delights, where the river itself becomes part of your dining experience. This charming establishment offers more than cuisine‚Äîit's a journey into Vietnam's agricultural heartland. Moored along the lush riverbanks of Can Tho, the restaurant gently sways with the water's rhythm, creating a meditative dining experience as sunset paints the river in shades of gold and crimson.

The menu celebrates the bounty of the Mekong Delta, featuring the freshest catches of the day and produce harvested from the surrounding waterways and orchards. Grilled fish prepared whole and wrapped in banana leaves arrives aromatic and tender. Fresh spring rolls are wrapped tableside using herbs picked from delta gardens that morning. The river snails, cooked in garlic and lemongrass, are a regional specialty that captures the essence of delta cooking. Seafood hot pot simmers with river prawns, squid, and fish in a broth infused with local ingredients.

As night falls and lanterns illuminate the water, diners become part of the river's story. Listen to the gentle lap of water against the boat, watch fishermen casting nets nearby, and taste flavors that only the Mekong Delta can offer. This is authentic dining connected to land, water, and tradition‚Äîa culinary pilgrimage to the soul of Vietnam.
