The Thunder Bolts

Welcome to The Thunder Bolts, the most electrifying dodgeball team in the multiverse! We've assembled a roster of legendary athletes, warriors, and strategic masterminds who bring unmatched speed, agility, and tactics to the court. When we step onto that arena, opponents know they're in for a storm of epic proportions. Meet our championship lineup!

Neo

Neo brings bullet-time reflexes to our team, making him virtually untouchable on the court. His ability to perceive incoming dodgeballs in slow motion and dodge with superhuman agility makes him our defensive anchor. Plus, his experience bending the rules of reality means he sees angles and opportunities others simply can't comprehend.

Wonder Woman

Diana Prince brings Amazonian strength and warrior precision to every throw. Her combat training and supernatural accuracy mean every ball she launches is a heat-seeking missile. With her lightning-fast reactions and unbreakable spirit, she's our offensive powerhouse who never backs down from a challenge.

Spider-Man

Peter Parker's spider-sense gives us an unfair advantage—he knows where every ball is going before it's even thrown! His wall-crawling abilities and acrobatic moves make him impossible to hit, and his web-slinging precision translates perfectly to pinpoint dodgeball accuracy. He's also great for team morale with his quick wit and friendly neighborhood spirit.

Sherlock Holmes

Holmes brings the strategic mastermind element to The Thunder Bolts. His deductive reasoning allows him to predict opponent patterns and weak points within seconds of the match starting. While others rely on physical prowess, Sherlock outthinks the competition, positioning our team perfectly and exploiting every tactical opening with calculated precision.

The Flash

Barry Allen is the ultimate wild card on our roster. With super-speed that breaks the laws of physics, he can catch multiple balls simultaneously, make impossible saves, and throw dodgeballs that appear to teleport across the court. Having a speedster means we essentially have five players doing the work of twenty. Game, set, and match—in under a second!
